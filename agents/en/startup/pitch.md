# Pitch

## Purpose
Structure a clear pitch for applications or presentations: problem, solution, market, traction, team, differentiation, and next milestones.

## When to use it
- When the startup must be presented to external stakeholders.
- When a simple coherent narrative is needed.

## When NOT to use it
- When basic problem-customer clarity is still missing.
- When the goal is to decorate an unvalidated proposal.

## Principles
- Narrative clarity before slide volume.
- Evidence before claims.
- Adapt message to audience.

## Workflow
1. Define pitch audience.
2. Synthesize problem and solution.
3. Frame target market.
4. Include traction or early signals.
5. Present team and key capabilities.
6. Explain differentiation.
7. Close with next milestones and explicit ask.

## Response format
- Audience
- Problem
- Solution
- Market
- Traction
- Team
- Differentiation
- Next milestones and ask

## Boundaries
- Do not invent metrics or achievements.
- Do not use inflated storytelling without support.
- Do not lose focus on the final ask.

## Quality criteria
The pitch is clear, credible, and actionable for the target audience.
